Sprint Guidelines

A shared reference for how we plan, estimate, track, and reflect on our work. The goal is consistency and clarity — not rigidity. Use this as a guide, not a rulebook.

🗂️ DA Catalyst Board ↗
01

Sprint Planning

Sprints kick off on Thursdays. Your sprint board should be finalized by Wednesday EOD — this is a hard deadline, not a suggestion. It gives Jo time to review and align with you before Thursday if needed.

💡 Ideally aim to have your board ready Wednesday morning so there's room for a quick sync if something needs adjusting.

When planning your sprint, make sure each ticket reflects real, intentional work. Ask yourself: does this sprint move my active projects forward — or am I just filling it with tickets?

02

Story Points & Time-Based Estimation

We use a Fibonacci-based story point system where each value maps to an explicit time range. This gives the team a shared language when estimating — so that a "3" means the same thing to everyone during planning.

⚠️ Estimates are guidelines, not commitments. Time is not a performance metric — story points are for planning, not measuring people.
Points Time Estimate Guidance
0.5~Half dayVery small, clearly scoped task. Be intentional — don't skip this in favour of defaulting to 1.
1~1 dayCan be picked up and completed within a single work day.
21–2 daysFits comfortably into 1–2 days with known scope and limited blockers.
32–3 daysSlightly more involved, may require coordination or validation steps.
5~1 weekLarger chunk spanning multiple stages. Consider splitting for pairing.
8Full sprintFills the majority of a sprint. Consider splitting across team members.
13Break it downToo large to estimate reliably. Must be decomposed before entering a sprint.

03

Sprint Expectations

Each analyst should enter a sprint with 8–10 story points of planned tickets. This is a planning target, not a closing guarantee — real work is unpredictable.

📋 If your board exceeds 13 points, work with Jo to deprioritize before the sprint kicks off.
🎯
Prioritize by urgency and impact. Don't close a ticket just because it's easy. Ad-hoc requests always sit behind planned initiatives unless there is true urgency.
🏖️
Account for PTO before the sprint starts. Plan fewer points for yourself upfront — PTO is not a retroactive explanation for incomplete work.
🔄
Carryover. Unclosed tickets roll over automatically when a new sprint starts. If a ticket has been carried over 2 sprints, reassess it with Jo — re-estimate, break it down, or deprioritize depending on the situation.
✅
The goal is to close all planned work by sprint end. In an ideal world. We know it's not always possible — but it's what we're aiming for.
04

Sprint Retro

We want retros to actually drive change — not be a checkbox. So we're running them at three levels of depth depending on what's needed.

Every Sprint
Async Burndown

Jo shares a burndown chart + quick insights in the Catalyst channel. Use the thread to drop thoughts async — no meeting required.

When needed
Standup Retro

First Friday Catalyst standup. Used when there's something worth discussing as a group. Not every sprint — only when there's signal.

Quarterly
Deep-Dive Session

A bigger retro / Figma exercise to surface patterns and make meaningful process improvements together as a team.

📌 If the burndown or async thread surfaces something worth acting on, we'll capture it with an owner. The quarterly session is where we more deliberately look for improvements and decide what to change.
05

Ticket Creation & CBT

A good ticket gives anyone on the team enough context to understand what's being done and why. Every ticket entering a sprint should have the following fields filled in:

✦ Description
What needs to be done and why. Enough context for someone else to understand the work.
✦ Story Points
Estimated using the Fibonacci scale. Must be set before the ticket enters a sprint.
✦ Team Tag
Tag the correct team so work is visible and attributed properly.
✦ Work Type Tag
Classify the type of work (e.g. analysis, data model, reporting, ad-hoc).

As you progress on a ticket, add comments to log updates — especially when blocked, when scope changes, or when you hand off work. Update your query in the Snowflake shared space and your output in Google Drive when complete.

🔧 CBT: Project and analytics epics are created and tagged automatically going forward — reducing manual setup. You still need to manually create your individual task tickets within those epics and add your own work details and estimates.
06

Escalation

The goal is always to close your planned work by end of sprint. When that's not possible, here's how to handle it — the key principle is communicate early, not at the end.

01
Urgent pop-up request
Communicate as soon as it's raised. Explain the impact to planned work in your daily standup thread or meeting. Escalate to Jo if you need support pushing things back.
02
External dependency delay
Log the blocker clearly on the ticket and call it out in your daily standup thread. Don't let it sit silently.
03
Ticket taking longer than expected
50%+ over estimate: flag it in your standup thread with context — what's the blocker, do you need support, what's the new timeline?

Doubling or more: stop and reassess. The ticket likely needs to be broken down, re-estimated, or deprioritized. Loop in Jo.
04
Planned PTO
Account for it before the sprint starts — plan fewer points for yourself upfront. PTO is not a retroactive explanation for incomplete work at sprint end.
